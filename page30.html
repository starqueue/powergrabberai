We start with what we cannot do. Most vendors end there by accident.

What PowerGrabber cannot do

These are real limitations. Not edge cases. Not items on a future roadmap. If you need coverage here, you need a different tool.

×
Read request or response bodies
PowerGrabber operates at the network-metadata level. It sees endpoints, headers, and byte counts. It never inspects prompt content or model output.
×
Assess prompt injection content
Without reading payloads, it cannot detect or score prompt injection attacks. You need an inline proxy or application-layer tool for that.
×
Score misinformation or hallucination
It has no access to model outputs. It cannot evaluate factual accuracy, bias, or hallucination rates.
×
Track local code execution
AI-generated code that runs locally is outside the network observation boundary. PowerGrabber sees the API call, not what happens after.
×
Reconcile exact costs
Cost figures are estimates based on token-count heuristics and published pricing. They will not match your invoice to the cent.
×
Identify individual users on shared machines
On multi-user hosts, it attributes traffic to the machine, not the person. Per-user attribution requires OS-level identity integration it does not provide.

What PowerGrabber can do

+
Endpoint detection
Identify every AI API endpoint contacted from your network. OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, Cohere, Replicate, HuggingFace, and 40+ others.
+
Cost estimation
Estimate spend per provider, team, and time period using token-count heuristics and published pricing tables.
+
Credential exposure detection
Detect API keys sent over unencrypted channels, keys present in headers to unexpected endpoints, and credential reuse patterns.
+
Data flow mapping
Map which internal systems talk to which AI providers, how often, and how much data flows in each direction.
+
OWASP LLM Top 10 scoring (9 of 20 items)
Assess 9 items from network metadata alone. The other 11 are marked NOT ASSESSABLE — not silently skipped.
+
Policy engine
Define rules: block providers, enforce encryption, cap spend, require approval for new endpoints. Enforce them at the network level.
+
Tool fingerprinting
Identify which AI tools (Copilot, Cursor, ChatGPT, Claude) are in use by their network signatures, without installing agents on endpoints.

NOT ASSESSABLE is a feature

Most compliance tools present a single score. 87/100. A green checkmark. A letter grade. They do not tell you which items were actually measured and which were assumed, inferred, or quietly skipped.

PowerGrabber scores 9 of 20 OWASP LLM Top 10 items from network metadata. The remaining 11 are explicitly marked NOT ASSESSABLE.

Those 8 NOT ASSESSABLE items are not failures. They are a map. They tell your security team exactly where to focus other tools — application-layer scanners, code review, red-team exercises. Instead of a false sense of coverage, you get an honest gap analysis.

A vendor who claims 100% coverage is either inspecting your data in ways you should question, or they are marking items as "pass" without evidence.

What Each Verdict Means as Audit Evidence

Each verdict carries specific meaning for auditors. Understanding these distinctions is critical for accurate audit documentation.

PASS

Control Operating Effectively

The assessed risk area has been monitored and no issues were detected within the observation window. Evidence supports a conclusion of effective operation.

WARNING

Control Weakness Identified

Indicators suggest a gap or potential issue. Not a confirmed failure, but sufficient evidence to warrant a management response and remediation plan.

FAIL

Control Failure

A confirmed violation of the assessed criteria. Requires immediate remediation and must be reported as an exception in audit findings.

NOT ASSESSABLE

Insufficient Visibility

The tool cannot assess this risk from its observation point. This is valuable — it tells auditors exactly where to focus other assessment methods such as vendor questionnaires, penetration testing, or manual review.

The Value of NOT ASSESSABLE

NOT ASSESSABLE is not a gap in coverage — it is a precise identification of where your other audit procedures should focus.

  1. ASI04 Knowledge Poisoning: Cannot be assessed from network traffic alone. Auditors should request model training documentation from AI providers and review data provenance controls.
  2. ASI05 Memory Poisoning: Requires inspection of tool-specific memory/context mechanisms. Auditors should evaluate each tool's context persistence architecture and isolation controls.
  3. ASI08 Hallucination: Output accuracy cannot be validated from traffic monitoring. Auditors should review human-in-the-loop processes and output validation procedures within business workflows.

Continuous Monitoring as Audit Evidence

SOC 2 and SOX both require evidence that controls operate continuously, not just at a point in time. PowerGrabber AI provides this through always-on monitoring.

Observation Window

Every assessment includes the exact time range monitored. Auditors can verify continuous coverage with no gaps. Default window: rolling 30 days with daily snapshots.

Control Effectiveness Over Time

Trend data shows whether a control has been consistently PASS, or if it oscillates between PASS and WARNING. Auditors can assess sustained vs. point-in-time compliance.

Gap Identification

When monitoring was interrupted (e.g., extension disabled, system restarted), PowerGrabber AI logs the gap with timestamps. Auditors see exactly when monitoring was and was not active.

Automated Alerting

Real-time alerts ensure that control failures are detected and communicated immediately, not discovered weeks later during a scheduled review. Alert timestamps prove timely detection.
